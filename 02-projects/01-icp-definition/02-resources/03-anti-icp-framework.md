## Anti-Persona Examples

### "Budget-Constrained Startup Steve"
- Pre-seed or seed stage
- <$500K ARR, <10 employees
- Looking for enterprise features at startup prices
- **Disqualify because**: Insufficient budget, rapid priority shifts, high churn risk

### "Feature-Demanding Enterprise Earl"
- Large enterprise (10,000+ employees)
- Complex 12-18 month procurement
- Expects extensive customization
- **Disqualify because**: Your product is standardized, not custom; high CAC, uncertain LTV

### "Wrong Use Case Wendy"
- Trying to use product for unintended purpose
- Misunderstands core value proposition
- **Disqualify because**: Will be frustrated, cannot achieve goals, guaranteed churn

## Train Reps: Disqualification Mindset

**Key Shift**: Instead of trying to qualify everyone, actively remove items that don't meet criteria.

**Benefits of saying "No"**:
- Gains instant credibility with prospect
- Shows honesty and business understanding
- Builds trust for future opportunities
- Frees time for high-probability deals

**Celebrate good disqualifications**, not just closed deals.
